Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks, Age and Income

Clicking various variables allows the comparison of relationships between them.

The following relationships seem to be indicated by the data.
Unsurprisingly, there seems to be a positive correlation between the percent in poverty and those lacking health care. Southern states tend to have the highest rates of poverty and less healthcare coverage.
Similarly, there appears to be a negative correlation between those lacking Healthcare and Household Income.
Smoking has a positive correlation with poverty and a negative correlation with household income.
Obesity also has a positive correlation with poverty and a negative correlation with household income.
